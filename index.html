Philosophy

My philosophy of learning and work can be summarised as:

  • Transparent - I believe that openness and honesty provide the best environment for true collaborative work.
  • Interactive - When creating workshops and learning materials, I generally design for interaction, feedback and dialogue - believing that this is more likely to encourage changes in behaviour and development of skills and knowledge.
  • User-centred - I have learnt that it’s essential to involve real users in the conversations around products from the beginning, even before the product is designed.
  • Lean - I am a strong advocate of the Lean principles successfully adopted in the manufacturing, software development and service industries.
