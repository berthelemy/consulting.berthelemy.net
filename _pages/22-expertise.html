Helping business leaders

Case study: CABI

I worked with the CABI Academy, over four years, to create an online learning capability that now serves over 28,000 learners from more than 140 countries.

This involved:

  • Gaining funding for each year's set of work packages
  • Developing a robust course delivery platform, based on Moodle
  • Creating a global skills framework for agriculture
  • Building a team to develop high-quality learning experiences and assessments
  • Creating a public data dashboard using PowerBI connected to the Moodle database
  • Piloting an AI-supported process to automatically interview participants and synthesise the feedback into project reports

Creating an effectve learning solution requires a complex iterative process, balancing the constraints and capabilities of the organisation's people, processes and technology.

This may involve:

  • Building out the business case and writing proposals
  • Identifying and configuring the technology platform(s)
  • Mapping out and designing integrations and operational processes
  • Specifying new functionality to be developed
  • Designing the overall approach to teaching, learning and assessment
  • Agreeing quality standards
  • Finding, training and coaching team members
  • Managing the project to deliver the solution
  • Creating data-driven websites to deliver communications and data dashboards

Clear, effective communication is at the heart of what I do - using a range of tools and techniques to ensure all stakeholders are consulted and informed as needed.

Helping product teams

Case study: Cadmore Media

I consulted with Cadmore Media to help them develop a comprehensive capability to deliver courses and assessments within their existing media suite for professional associations.

The assignment included:

  • Analysis of client requirements against their existing systems and against Cadmore Media's existing offering
  • Competition analysis against other providers used by Cadmore Media's clients
  • Development of a prioritised product roadmap
  • Consultancy support for the engineers to explore a range of elearning standards, including SCORM and xAPI

The market for learning & development software is wide, varied and complex. There are many acronyms, technical specifications, standards and conventions.

I help product teams to navigate this space:

  • Identifying the problem you're trying to solve
  • Explaining the various acronyms that may impinge on your product, such as SCORM, xAPI, LTI, LCMS, LXP etc
  • Assessing your product against the competition, and against the market's expectations - in terms of market fit, accessibility, usability and functionality
  • Building business cases for future developments
  • Creating prioritised roadmaps of new capabilities
  • Writing user stories and acceptance criteria
  • Managing the product development process

Throughout, my aim is to ensure clear communication between product teams, learning specialists and the business leaders.
